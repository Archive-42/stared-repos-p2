Personal info:

I was born and raised in the Bay Area -- living in Cupertino, Oakland, Morgan Hill, San Jose, Santa Cruz, and now Mountain View. When I was very young I developed a strong affinity for self-teaching. I taught myself to read, to play piano, sing, and hear harmony, and have always loved science and math. I also started gaming at a young age -- starting with PC games and then moving on to console games. In school I was a three-sport athlete (football, basketball, and track) with a strong interest and aptitude for music, Physics, problem solving, and creativity. After graduating SJSU I taught for 11 years. Subjects I taught included: Music (running a middle school music program), Physics, Engineering, Math, and Astronomy. Since leaving teaching in 2013 I started my own private tutoring business which has been pretty successful. I am now pursuing a degree in Software Engineering with a concentration in Gaming and Web and Mobile development. I have always been passionate about how things work, troubleshooting, solving problems, and been self-motivated. I also love working with all different types of people as well as learning and contributing as much as possible in professional situations. I have developed expertise as a vocalist, arranger, and producer, as a Physicist, a teacher, and now as a Software Developer. I have a dedication to excellence in everything I do. Feel free to browse my portfolio if you would like to see some projects I have created or have been a part of. Click here to link to my Full Stack Web portfolio. My most recent music project is an EP called "Eyes on Me" with an all-vocal/acapella group called Business Casual. Goals:

My professional goals at this point can be separated into short and long-term.

My short term goals include finishing my degree in Software Engineering and learning as much as I can about coding, software development, and game development. I want to have a broad base of skills and languages, as well as a strong specialization in Front End Web Development. Some of my past projects include slowly building my own Physics Engine in my spare time, designing and coding my own portfolio website (this one using HTML/CSS/JavaScript), making a 3D pong game (JavaScript), making an interactive slide show (JavaScript), developing an RPG/puzzle game to be released in mid to late 2015 on iOS and Android but developed using the Unity Engine, and coding at Sony PlayStation for 4 different PS4 titles.

My long term goals include landing an initial position in game development as a Graphics or Physics programmer and eventually being able to specialize in Physics system development. This could either be building an in-house Physics/Graphics system at a gaming company or working with popular middle-ware (Unity, PhysX, Havok, Bullet Physics, etc.) and integrating it into the larger game engine. I am also interested in becoming a Full Stack Web Developer in gaming and non-gaming settings.
